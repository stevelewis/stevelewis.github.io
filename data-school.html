The Data School by Chartio

Marketing in today's world is different. Sure, we still have static ads in subways, but there are so many more ways a company can get in front of potential customers. The Data School is one such example.

The Data School logo I created for Chartio's new educational content site.

Written content was the most successful channel for new leads at Chartio, but this project also grew out of a genuine desire to share things the Chartio team had learned working in the industry. We created an open-sourced micro site with content grouped into living web books.

The original Wordpress site I designed.
Wireframes of version 2.
More iterating.
Introducing the idea of grouping articles into web books.

Data School was like creating a whole new product. The site used the same core typeface Chartio always has, Open Sans, but I gave the content and covers their own unique type to set this content apart.

The final dataschool.com homepage.
An article/chapter. The sidebar could be hidden away for clutter-free reading.

Readers could also leave simple feedback and suggestions on chapters or join a Slack group. I was also responsible for the development of the site and training other teammates on how to make different updates.
